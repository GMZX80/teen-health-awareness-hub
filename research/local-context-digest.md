## School Avoidance And Anxiety Resources

High-value starting points:

- YoungMinds school anxiety and refusal.
- Anna Freud school attendance and mental wellbeing.
- IPSEA school anxiety where SEND or education rights questions arise.
- NHS anxiety in children and NHS anxiety disorders in children.
- NICE social anxiety guideline when symptoms strongly fit fear of social judgement or scrutiny.

Practical use:

- Build a school meeting agenda from specific barriers, not general worry.
- Pair emotional validation with small, planned steps.
- Ask school for one named trusted adult, a safe route to signal distress, and a review date.
- Keep nausea/body symptoms in the same picture without assuming they are only anxiety.

## Nausea And Physical Symptoms

NHS cyclical vomiting syndrome was included mainly for red-flag thinking. It should not be used to self-diagnose.

Practical use:

- Track nausea timing, vomiting, pain, hydration, sleep, food, school demands, panic symptoms, and what helps.
- Ask a GP what physical causes should be ruled out.
- Seek urgent help for red flags such as severe pain, vomiting blood, green vomit in a child, stiff neck, severe headache, dehydration, or serious illness.

## Crisis And Safety Sources

Use NHS urgent mental health help and NHS self-harm help as primary safety references. Mind's helpline directory should be opened manually because automated retrieval was blocked.

Practical use:

- Keep urgent routes separate and easy to find.
- Do not wait for routine support if there is immediate risk of harm, overdose, serious injury, or inability to stay safe.
