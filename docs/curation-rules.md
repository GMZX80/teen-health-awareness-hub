# Curation Rules

Use this checklist before adding advice to the main site.

## Source Quality

- Prefer NHS, NICE, government, recognised clinical bodies, established charities, and school/education rights organisations.
- Record the URL, publisher, access date, and what the source is actually saying.
- Separate clinical guidance from lived experience, opinion, product marketing, and social media posts.
- Do not copy long passages. Summarise and link.
- Re-check older advice before treating it as current.

## Privacy

- Do not commit names, school names, clinician names, phone numbers, email addresses, appointment dates, screenshots, private messages, or detailed personal history.
- Keep private logs outside the public site unless they are anonymised and deliberately reviewed.
- Treat GitHub issues as unsuitable for sensitive health details unless the repo remains private and the owner explicitly accepts that risk.

## Advice Handling

- Convert broad advice into a cautious next step, not a rule.
- Note uncertainty and what would change the plan.
- Do not frame a young person's distress as manipulation, laziness, or defiance.
- Do not assume nausea is anxiety until physical causes have been considered appropriately.
- Escalate safety concerns immediately rather than storing them as research notes.
